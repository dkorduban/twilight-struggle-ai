## Key Findings

1. **Mode balance:** Humans play Influence 40.3%, Event 34.6%, Coup 14.2%, Realignment 2.0%, Space 8.2%
   - Our model over-coups at ~39% vs human 14.2%
   - Humans event much more (34.6%) than a coup-heavy model would suggest

2. **Coup targets:** Humans mostly coup in:
   - Africa: 35.0%
   - CentralAmerica: 21.6%
   - SouthAmerica: 17.6%

3. **Influence allocation:** Humans spread influence rather than stacking:
   - 2 ops: most common shape (2,) = 50.8%
   - 3 ops: most common shape (2, 1) = 46.7%
   - 4 ops: most common shape (2, 1, 1) = 31.9%

4. **Europe in Early War:** Humans place influence in Europe during Early War:
   - USSR: 122 inf (12% of Early War influence)
   - US: 281 inf (22% of Early War influence)

## Implications for Training

1. **Coup penalty/reward shaping:** Model over-coups at 39% vs human ~14%. Consider adding coup frequency as an auxiliary loss or reward penalty.
2. **Event firing:** Humans fire events much more often. The model should learn when events are strategically valuable, not just default to ops.
3. **Europe focus:** If the model ignores Europe in Early War, it diverges from human strategy. Europe scoring dominance is critical in competitive play.
4. **Influence spreading:** Humans often split influence across 2-3 countries rather than stacking all ops in one. This diversification reduces risk.
5. **Space race as dump:** Humans space ~8% of actions — a meaningful escape valve for opponent cards. Model should learn this pattern.
