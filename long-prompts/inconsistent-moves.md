Here is the list I meant. I’m counting only clear contradictions with standard card text / standard Twilight Struggle rules, not blank outputs on persistent or hidden-state cards. I’m also applying the normal rule 5.2: if a player uses an opponent-associated card for Operations, that opponent event still occurs unless a prerequisite or superseding event prevents it.  

## Event cards

1. **Captured Nazi Scientist** — Turn 1 AR0 gives the US **+2 VP and +1 Space Race**. Standard Captured Nazi Scientist only advances the Space Race by 1. ([Twilight Strategy][1]) 

2. **Five Year Plan** — Turn 1 AR4 resolves as **USSR -1 East Germany, -1 Poland**. Standard Five Year Plan makes the USSR randomly discard a card; it is not an influence-removal event. ([Twilight Strategy][1]) 

3. **NATO** — Turn 2 AR0 plays NATO as a live event before **Marshall Plan** or **Warsaw Pact Formed** had been played. Standard NATO requires one of those cards to have been played first. ([Twilight Strategy][1]) 

4. **Independent Reds** — Turn 3 AR2 adds **+1 US influence to five different Eastern European countries**. Standard Independent Reds equalizes US influence in **one** eligible country only. ([Twilight Strategy][1]) 

5. **UN Intervention** — Turn 3 AR4 resolves as direct USSR influence removal, and Turn 7 AR3 resolves as direct US influence placement. Standard UN Intervention must be paired with an opponent event card and uses that other card’s Ops; it is not a standalone influence event. ([Twilight Strategy][1])  

6. **Warsaw Pact Formed** — Turn 3 AR6 correctly removes US influence in Eastern Europe, but then also **improves DEFCON to 5**. That DEFCON effect is not on the card. ([Twilight Strategy][1]) 

7. **Nixon Plays the China Card** — Turn 4 AR0 gives the US **2 VP only**. Standard Nixon transfers the China Card to the US face down if the USSR still has it, and the China Card normally starts with the USSR. ([Twilight Strategy][1]) 

8. **Brush War** — Turn 4 AR1 in Cameroon and Turn 8 AR0 in Lebanon both resolve as **+3 influence and +2 VP**. Standard Brush War gives **1 VP**, replaces opponent influence in the target, and adds 3 Military Ops; it does not simply add 3 fresh influence like that. ([Twilight Strategy][1])  

9. **South African Unrest** — Turn 4 AR2 and Turn 9 AR6 both give **+2 South Africa and +2 to one adjacent country**. Standard South African Unrest is either **+2 South Africa** or **+1 South Africa +2 to one adjacent country**. ([Twilight Strategy][1])  

10. **Missile Envy** — Turn 4 AR2 turns into raw US influence placement, and Turn 9 AR4 appears to do essentially nothing. Standard Missile Envy exchanges itself for the opponent’s highest-Ops card and forces the opponent to spend Missile Envy for Ops next action round. ([Twilight Strategy][1])  

11. **Nuclear Subs** — Turn 5 AR1 becomes **US +1 in Cameroon**. Standard Nuclear Subs only changes how US coups in battlegrounds affect DEFCON for the rest of the turn. ([Twilight Strategy][1]) 

12. **Grain Sales to Soviets** — Turn 5 AR1 and Turn 7 AR2 become raw US influence placement. Standard Grain Sales is a random-card effect on the USSR hand, with a fallback to using 2 Ops only if the card is returned or the USSR has no cards. ([Twilight Strategy][1])  

13. **Bear Trap** — Turn 5 AR2 becomes **US +1 in Panama**. Standard Bear Trap traps the USSR’s future action rounds until it discards a 2+ Ops card and passes the die roll. ([Twilight Strategy][1]) 

14. **Our Man in Tehran** — Turn 5 AR6 becomes **US +1 in Pakistan**. Standard Our Man in Tehran is the five-card look / discard / reshuffle effect if the US controls at least one Middle East country. ([Twilight Strategy][1]) 

15. **Socialist Governments** — Turn 5 AR7 includes a **US +1 in Panama**. Standard Socialist Governments only removes a total of 3 US influence from Western Europe; Panama has no place in that resolution. ([Twilight Strategy][1]) 

16. **Junta** — Turn 6 AR2 has a plausible **+2 Dominican Republic**, but then also shows **US -3 in Panama**. Standard Junta adds 2 influence to one Central/South American country and may then make a free coup or realignment using 2 Ops; the logged Panama line is not a normal Junta event resolution. ([Twilight Strategy][1]) 

17. **We Will Bury You** — Turn 6 AR5 and Turn 7 AR4 award the USSR **+3 VP immediately**. Standard We Will Bury You delays that 3 VP unless UN Intervention is played as an event on the US’s next action round. ([Twilight Strategy][1])  

18. **Arab-Israeli War** — Turn 6 AR7 and Turn 10 AR7 play Arab-Israeli War after **Camp David Accords** had already been played on Turn 6 AR0, which standard text explicitly forbids. The logged effects at those later plays also do not resemble the normal war resolution. ([Twilight Strategy][1])  

19. **Special Relationship** — Turn 7 AR6 places US influence in **Mexico and Indonesia** and gives 2 VP. Standard Special Relationship allows either one **UK-adjacent** country without NATO, or one **Western Europe** country plus 2 VP with NATO. ([Twilight Strategy][1]) 

20. **Che** — Turn 7 AR6 gives raw USSR influence in **Argentina** and **Tunisia**. Standard Che authorizes coup attempts only, and only in **non-battleground** countries in Central America, South America, or Africa. ([Twilight Strategy][1]) 

21. **Defectors** — Turn 8 AR5, played by the USSR during an action round, gives the US **2 VP**. Standard says that action-round USSR play gives the US **1 VP**. ([Twilight Strategy][1]) 

22. **Blockade** — Turn 8 AR5, used by the US for a coup, never applies the West Germany discard / wipe check. Under standard rules, because Blockade is an opponent-associated event, it should still resolve unless prevented; and its text is about West Germany, not Ethiopia. ([Twilight Strategy][1]) 

23. **The Iron Lady** — Turn 8 AR6 shows only **US +1 VP**. Standard Iron Lady also adds **1 USSR influence to Argentina** and removes **all USSR influence from the UK**. ([Twilight Strategy][1]) 

24. **Indo-Pakistani War** — Turn 9 AR0 removes **7 US influence from Pakistan** but leaves only **2 USSR influence** there. Standard on success replaces **all** opponent influence in the target with the winner’s influence. ([Twilight Strategy][1]) 

25. **Shuttle Diplomacy** — Turn 9 AR7 becomes Soviet influence placement, MilOps resets, and **US +2 VP**. Standard Shuttle Diplomacy only modifies the next Middle East or Asia scoring card. ([Twilight Strategy][1]) 

26. **Latin American Debt Crisis** — Turn 10 AR1 becomes **USSR +3 Cuba and +1 Guatemala**. Standard Latin American Debt Crisis forces the US to discard a 3+ Ops card or else allows the USSR to double its influence in **two South American countries**. ([Twilight Strategy][1]) 

## Scoring cards with extra side effects that are not on the cards

27. **Asia Scoring** — Turn 2 AR6 also **improves DEFCON to 5**. Standard Asia Scoring is only regional scoring. ([Twilight Strategy][1]) 

28. **Africa Scoring** — Turn 4 AR7 improves DEFCON, and Turn 7 AR7 improves DEFCON and resets Soviet MilOps. Standard Africa Scoring is only regional scoring. ([Twilight Strategy][1])  

29. **Southeast Asia Scoring** — Turn 5 AR7 also **improves DEFCON to 3**. Standard Southeast Asia Scoring only awards VP by controlled countries. ([Twilight Strategy][1]) 

30. **Central America Scoring** — Turn 7 AR7 also gives **US +1 in the UK**. Standard Central America Scoring only scores Central America. ([Twilight Strategy][1]) 

31. **Europe Scoring** — Turn 8 AR7 also **improves DEFCON to 4** and resets **US Military Ops to 0**. Standard Europe Scoring only scores Europe. ([Twilight Strategy][1]) 

The biggest ones, in practical game-impact terms, are Captured Nazi Scientist, Nixon/China handling, Brush War, We Will Bury You, Defectors, Indo-Pakistani War, and the scoring-card side effects.
